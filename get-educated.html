The Brain


more

The brain is made up of many parts that all work together as a team. Each of these different parts has a specific and important job to do. When drugs enter the brain, they interfere with its normal tasks and can eventually lead to changes in how well it works.



The Adolescent Brain


Brain Stem: Controls all functions the body needs to stay alive (breathing, heart rate, body temperature, sleep cycle) & lets the brain know what’s happening to the body → Drugs can cause breathing to dangerously slow and even stop (overdose)


Limbic System: Links together brain structures that control emotional responses & motivates to repeat behaviors → Drugs can disrupt the modulation of feelings and motivations of the unconscious


Cerebral Cortex: Processes information from the senses & powers the ability to think, make decisions, and control actions → Drugs can interfere with the ability to make good judgments and resist impulses



When drugs (chemicals) enter the body, they tap into the brain’s communication system and tamper with the way information is sent, received, and processed.

Different drugs work in the brain by: imitating the brain’s natural chemical messengers & overstimulating the “reward circuit” of the brain.



The Reward Circuit


Drug use can lead to serious changes in the brain that affect how a person thinks and acts. This impaired brain development may lead to risky behavior, challenges in emotional development, and even mental health disorders.


Drug use can also cause medical problems including: heart disease, lung problems, cancer, and HIV/AIDS. Drug use can even result in death.


Drug use in youth increases the chances of experimenting with other drugs and even addiction.



In adults, brain cells are protected with myelin which helps brain messages travel. However, in teens, brain cells are still maturing and are not fully protected by myelin. This results in more intense, less refined messages being sent and received. Due to this, teens experience more powerful sensations of pleasure from the use of drugs, and the brain's reward systems are triggered more powerfully. This puts youth at greater risk for addiction.



The Addiction Process


The Pleasure Effect: Drugs take control of the brain’s reward system, releasing large amounts of dopamine. The brain remembers this feeling & sends out an intense motivation to seek out and use drugs again.

​

The Repeat Effect: After repeated drug use, the brain starts to adjust to the surges of dopamine. As a result, the ability to feel pleasure is reduced. In order to compensate for this, drug intake is increased, and the toxic cycle continues. Now the person needs drugs to just feel normal.



Addiction is a chronic brain disease that causes a person to take drugs repeatedly, despite the harm it may cause. Drugs often start as a recreational activity; however, repeated drug use can alter the brain, driving a person to make impulsive choices, regardless of the consequences.





Common Drugs



In the world of substance use and addiction, only one thing is certain: it will never stop changing. As old drugs fade away, new generations of products continue to emerge. Moreover, substances will continue to pose a threat to the physical and mental well-being of youth if the environment is not changed.



E-CIGARETTES & TOBACCO



Nicotine is the chemical found in the tobacco plant. Usually cigarettes, cigars, hookahs, dried, or flavor. Can be smoked, inhaled, eaten, or snorted.

Change a person’s awareness of their surroundings, thoughts, and feelings. Usually comes from plants, mushrooms, or synthetic. Can be swallowed, snorted, or brewed.

Medications related to testosterone that are made in labs. Usually pills, liquids, gels, or creams. Can be swallowed, injected, or applied on the skin.

SALVIA



A herb in the mint family. Usually dried. Can be eaten, smoked, inhaled, or swallowed.

HEROIN



Made from morphine (poppy). Usually white or brown powder, or a black, sticky substance (black tar). Can be injected, sniffed, smoked, or snorted.

SPICE



A mix of herbs and laboratory-made chemicals. Usually dried (herbal) or a powder. Can be smoked, swallowed, or inhaled.

COCAINE



Drug made from the leaves of the coca plant. Usually a white powder or rock crystal. Can be snorted, injected, rubbed into gums above the teeth, and inhaled through vapors.
